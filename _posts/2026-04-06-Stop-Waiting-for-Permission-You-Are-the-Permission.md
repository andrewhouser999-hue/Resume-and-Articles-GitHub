title: "Stop Waiting for Permission — You Are the Permission"

There is a version of your life you have been circling for months, maybe years. You know what it looks like. You have thought about it more times than you can count. And yet you have not moved.

Not because you don't know what to do. Not because you lack the ability. Because somewhere along the way, you decided that this kind of move requires permission, and you have been waiting for someone to give it to you.

In my twenties and thirties, people would pull me aside in ways I never solicited. They would share things with me, intimate things, important things, and then look up halfway through, almost surprised at themselves. Others came to me for guidance. A few described me, in their own words, as a youth pastor, though I held no title and no pulpit. My deepest interests were always in how people think, relate, connect and socialize. I was drawn to it the way some people are drawn to music or numbers, not as a career calculation but as something that felt native to who I was.

And then I laid it down. Not once, but repeatedly. Time would free up, the interest would resurface, and then life would fill back in around it and I would set it aside again, quietly, without ceremony. What I did not fully recognize until much later was that something else was running underneath all of that, an undercurrent, a resistance. A belief that sounded like smallness and said, simply, *not me. Someone better.*

That belief cost me years.

**The Permission Myth**

Most people don't recognize they're doing this. It doesn't feel like waiting for permission. It feels like being responsible, realistic, not wanting to be reckless or arrogant or naive.

It sounds like *once I have more experience, then I'll go for it,* or *I just need someone to tell me this is actually a good idea,* or *when the timing is better, I'll make my move.*

These are not strategic pauses. They are permission requests dressed up as planning.

The timing will not get better on its own. The experience gap will not close before you start. And the validation you're waiting for, from a mentor, a market, an opportunity that drops in your lap, is not coming. Not because you don't deserve it, but because that's not how permission works.

**Who Gave You the Idea You Needed It?**

At some point early in life, you learned that forward motion required approval. A parent, a teacher, a system taught you to raise your hand before you spoke, to wait for the green light before you went, to earn the right to proceed.

That lesson was appropriate then. You were a child. You needed guardrails.

But you carried the lesson into adulthood and applied it to places it was never meant to go, your career, your relationships, your creative work, your sense of who you are allowed to become. And now you are a capable adult, sitting with a hand raised, waiting for a teacher who is not in the room.

The question that finally broke through for me was this: *who is better than the one with the inner calling, the passion, the tenacity, the acquired knowledge and the growing experience?*

I had no good answer. Neither will you.

**What the High-Permission People Know**

Look closely at the people you admire, the ones building things, leading rooms, creating work that matters. They are not more talented than you. They are not more certain than you. Most of them will tell you, if you ask honestly, that they felt under-qualified, underprepared and more than a little afraid.

The difference is not confidence. It is the decision that their own judgment is sufficient to begin.

They stopped outsourcing the green light. They stopped treating forward motion as something that had to be earned from an external authority. They moved, and let the movement produce the evidence they needed.

That is not arrogance. That is ownership.

**The Cost of Waiting**

Here is what permission-waiting actually costs you.

It costs you time you cannot get back. Every year spent waiting for conditions that were never going to self-assemble is a year that belonged to the version of you that decided to go.

It costs you credibility, with yourself. Every time you talk yourself out of a move you knew was right, you send a message to your own nervous system: *my judgment cannot be trusted.* That message compounds. It gets louder. The waiting produces more waiting.

And it costs the people around you. The thing you are withholding, your leadership, your voice, your creative contribution, your full presence in a relationship, is not a private loss. The people in your life feel the gap where you were supposed to show up.

**You Are the Authority**

The permission you have been waiting for is not coming from outside you because it was never located there.

Accepting that truth is not a passive moment. It is a decision that requires work, shaping your identity around it, building commitment that survives resistance, and making yourself fit for the journey ahead. That is not a one-time breakthrough. It is a practice.

You are the one who knows your values. You are the one who has lived your experiences, developed your instincts and built your judgment over years of paying attention. No external authority has access to that interior data. No mentor, no market signal, no perfect moment can tell you what you already know.

The move you have been postponing? Your gut already knows it's right. That's why it keeps coming back.

**What Happens When You Decide**

Permission-granting is not a dramatic moment. It does not feel like a lightning bolt. It feels quieter than that, more like a settling, like something that was tense finally releasing.

And then you move. Imperfectly, without full certainty, without all the experience you think you need. You move, and the movement teaches you things that no amount of waiting could have.

The clarity you were waiting for before you started? It shows up after you begin. Every time.

You do not need another credential, another endorsement, another sign that the moment is right.

You need to decide that your judgment, imperfect and incomplete as it is, is sufficient to take the next step.

It is.

Stop waiting.
